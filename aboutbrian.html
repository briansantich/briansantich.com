About Brian

I grew up in Chico, California and attended UC Berkeley for undergrad. After completing my studies at UC Berkeley, I moved to Washington, DC to work at the National Institutes of Health (NIH) for two years, before starting my Ph.D thesis work at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center (MSKCC) in New York City. I am currently a full time graduate student in the field of immuno-oncology. My work focuses on developing next generation therapies for pediatric cancers, such as neuroblastoma, but also extends into identifying the mechanisms of immunotherapies and reengineering anti-tumor therapies to various other cancer types.
